Cheyenne's Story

Cheyenne was a fifteen old Appalossa mare living in Omaha, Nebraska and sold several times during her life. Cheyenne was rescued by Carolina by February 2013. Carolina's biggest dream was to have a horse and become best friends with her. Cheyenne completed this dream for Carolina and so much more. Chey taught Carolina how to ride, train, and be close with a horse.

Learn what Chey loves most

Kindness

Cheyenne always showed kindnes and tenderness in her actions and behavior. She was gentle when walking near children and lowered her head to allow them to pet her. She was aware of her size and was cautious wherever she went, espcially when carrying a rider. Her unique personal ways of caring towards Carolina made her the once in a lifetime type of horse and love.

Learn more how to be kind to Chey

Love

The love between Carolina and Cheyenne was strong. Carolina cared and loved Cheyenne despite a medical conditional called COPD. Chey required special expensive treatments and shots monthly to keep her comfortable and healthy. Their love carried them through many trips to lakes, walking trails, friendships, and a marriage. Sadly, Chey eventually passed away from this disease in April 2015.

Don't Be Shy

Chey is short for Cheyenne. She was the most beautiful, magical horse. She was loving and graceful. She loved her family as much as they cherished her.

  • Carolina was given Cheyenne to take care of. At first, Carolina was shy with Chey.
  • Chey taught Carolina the things she liked most.
  • After doing these favorite activities, Carolina was not shy anymore. She loved spending time with Chey.
  • ...being brushed.
  • ...getting bathes.
  • ...eating her grain.
  • ...receiving treats.
  • ...riding in the pasture.
  • ...taking pictures.
  • ...even selfies.
  • ...most of all, eating treats!
  • Soon after Carolina introduced Chey to Ryan, her husband. Sure enough, Ryan was told, "Don't be shy with Chey."
